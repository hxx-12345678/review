## Part 2: The NEPQ Sales Conversation (Jeremy Miner)

### The 5 Stages Applied to BEYONDVYU

#### Stage 1: Connection (First 30 seconds)

**Goal:** Disarm the prospect, take focus off you, put it on them.

**Cold Call Opener:**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from BEYONDVYU. I know you're busy running [Restaurant Name], so I'll be brief. I was looking at your Google reviews and noticed something interesting — but I'm not even sure if this is a priority for you right now. Would you be completely opposed to a quick 2-minute conversation?"

**Why this works (NEPQ):**
- "I know you're busy" — disarms, shows empathy
- "I'll be brief" — sets expectation
- "I'm not even sure if this is a priority" — lowers guard, creates curiosity
- "Would you be completely opposed to..." — negative question flip, brain naturally says "no" to being opposed, so they say "no, I'm not opposed" = yes

**Warm Call / Referral Opener:**
> "[Referral Name] mentioned you might be interested in how we helped them manage their reviews. I was looking at your Google profile and noticed something interesting — but I'm not sure if this is the right time to discuss. Would you be completely opposed to a quick 3-minute conversation?"

---

### Stage 2: Engagement (Situation + Problem Awareness)

**Goal:** 80% prospect talking, 20% you. Build the gap.

#### Situation Questions (Current State)

> "Walk me through how you currently handle online reviews. Do you respond to them yourself, or does someone on your team handle it?"

> "How many reviews do you get in a typical week across Google, Zomato, and Swiggy?"

> "What do you do when you get a negative review? Walk me through your process."

> "How do you know if your online reputation is helping or hurting your business?"

> "Do you track what your competitors are doing with their reviews?"

#### Problem Awareness Questions

> "What's the biggest challenge with your current approach to reviews?"

> "If you had to guess, how many potential customers are you losing because of unmanaged reviews?"

> "What happens when a bad review sits unanswered for a week?"

> "How do you know if your rating is going up or down month-over-month?"

> "What's the hardest part about responding to reviews consistently?"

#### Consequence Questions (Build the Gap)

> "If nothing changes and you keep handling reviews the way you do now, where do you think your online reputation will be in 12 months?"

> "What's it costing you in lost customers every month because of unanswered negative reviews?"

> "Your competitor [Name] has been responding to every review and their rating has gone up. If they keep doing that and you don't, what happens to your market share?"

> "How much time does your team spend each week on review management? What could they be doing with that time instead?"

#### Solution Awareness Questions (Future State)

> "In a perfect world, what would your online reputation look like 6 months from now?"

> "If you could wave a magic wand, how would you want customers to find and choose your restaurant online?"

> "What would it mean for your business if you had 2,000 positive reviews instead of 500?"

> "If you could see exactly what your competitors are doing with their reviews, how would that change your strategy?"

### Stage 4: Presentation (Tailored to Their Gap)

**Goal:** Present only what solves THEIR specific problems. No feature dumps.

**Structure:**
1. **Acknowledge their gap:** "You mentioned [problem 1], [problem 2], and [problem 3]."
2. **Show the bridge:** "Here's how BEYONDVYU addresses each one."
3. **Ask, don't tell:** "Does this look like it would solve that issue for you?"

**Example (for a Review Giant like Grand Thakar):**
> "You mentioned three things: (1) you can't respond to thousands of reviews manually, (2) you don't know what competitors are doing, and (3) you can't spot trends in your feedback. Let me show you how BEYONDVYU handles each one.
>
> [Show AI response feature] — This lets you respond to 100 reviews in 5 minutes. You review, approve, and we handle the rest. Does this look like it would solve your response problem?
>
> [Show competitor monitoring] — Here's what [Competitor]'s customers are saying. You can see their strengths and weaknesses. How would having this data change your strategy?
>
> [Show sentiment trends] — This shows you exactly what customers are saying month over month. You can spot problems before they become crises. Would this be useful for your team?"

#### Stage 5: Commitment

**Goal:** Guide them to their own conclusion. Don't close — help them commit.

> "Based on everything we've discussed, do you feel like BEYONDVYU could help you achieve the reputation goals we talked about?"

> "What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable moving forward?"

> "If we could get you set up in 15 minutes and have your first review response going today, would that make sense to start with?"

**The "Suppose Question" for Commitment:**
> "Suppose we could get you set up today with your first QR code on tables, AI responses ready, and competitor tracking active — would you be ready to start the free trial?"

**Handling "I need to think about it":**
> "I understand. What specifically would you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?"

**Handling "I need to discuss with my partner/team":**
> "That makes sense. What concerns do you think they might have? If we could address those now, would that help you feel more confident moving forward?"

## Part 3: The Gap Selling Framework (Shelby Sapp / Keenan)

### The 3-Bucket Question System

Shelby Sapp's framework is the simplest and most actionable for day-to-day sales conversations. It maps directly to Gap Selling.

#### Bucket 1: Probing Questions (Current State)

**Goal:** Gather surface-level information. Understand where they are now.

> "How do you currently handle online reviews for [Restaurant Name]?"

> "How many reviews do you get in a typical month?"

> "What's your current rating on Google? On Zomato? On Swiggy?"

> "Who on your team is responsible for managing reviews?"

> "How much time do you spend on review management each week?"

> "Do you currently track what your competitors are doing?"

#### Bucket 2: Provoking Questions (Emotional Pain)

**Goal:** Double-click on their answers. Surface the emotional pain underneath.

> "You mentioned you get about 50 reviews a month. How many of those are negative? What does that feel like when you see a bad review?"

> "You said you spend about 5 hours a week on reviews. What else could you be doing with that time? How does that affect your ability to focus on the food and customers?"

> "When a bad review goes unanswered for a week, what happens? How does that affect your team's morale?"

> "You mentioned your competitor has more reviews than you. How does that make you feel about your market position?"

> "What's the real cost of not having a system for this? Not just in money, but in stress, time, and missed opportunities?"

#### Bucket 3: Future State Questions

> "In a perfect world, what would your online reputation look like 6 months from now?"

> "If this partnership works out perfectly, what does success look like for you? What number would you open your laptop and think: that's success?"

> "Why that number? What does [X] reviews or [X] rating actually mean to you personally?"

> "What would it feel like to never worry about a bad review again because you have a system that handles it?"

> "If you could see exactly what your competitors are doing and know exactly what customers are saying — how would that change how you run your business?"

## Part 6: The Gap Selling Framework (Shelby Sapp / Keenan)

### The 3-Bucket Question System

Shelby Sapp's framework is the simplest and most actionable for day-to-day sales conversations. It maps directly to Gap Selling.

#### Bucket 1: Probing Questions (Current State)

**Goal:** Gather surface-level information. Understand where they are now.

> "How do you currently handle online reviews for [Restaurant Name]?"

> "How many reviews do you get in a typical month?"

> "What's your current rating on Google? On Zomato? On Swiggy?"

> "Who on your team is responsible for managing reviews?"

> "How much time do you spend on review management each week?"

> "Do you currently track what your competitors are doing?"

#### Bucket 2: Provoking Questions (Emotional Pain)

**Goal:** Double-click on their answers. Surface the emotional pain underneath.

> "You mentioned you get about 50 reviews a month. How many of those are negative? What does that feel like when you see a bad review?"

> "You said you spend about 5 hours a week on reviews. What else could you be doing with that time? How does that affect your ability to focus on the food and customers?"

> "When a bad review goes unanswered for a week, what happens? How does that affect your team's morale?"

> "Your competitor [Name] has been responding to every review and their rating has gone up. How does that make you feel about your market position?"

> "What's the real cost of not having a system for this? Not just in money, but in stress, time, and missed opportunities?"

#### Bucket 3: Future State Questions

> "In a perfect world, what would your online reputation look like 6 months from now?"

> "If this partnership works out perfectly, what does success look like for you? What number would you open your laptop and think: that's success?"

> "Why that number? What does [X] reviews or [X] rating actually mean to you personally?"

> "What would it feel like to never worry about a bad review again because you have a system that handles it?"

> "If you could see exactly what your competitors are doing and know exactly what customers are saying — how would that change how you run your business?"

## Part 6: The 80/20 Rule — Shut Up and Listen

### The Most Important Sales Skill

Both Jeremy Miner and Shelby Sapp emphasize the same principle: **the prospect should talk 80% of the time, you talk 20%.**

**What this means in practice:**
- Every time you want to make a statement, turn it into a question
- After asking a question, shut up and wait for the answer
- Silence is powerful — let them fill the space
- Listen to what they MEAN, not just what they SAY

**Tonality (Shelby Sapp):**
- Speak with "periods" not "commas" — clear, distinct statements with downward inflection
- Mirror the last 3 words of their sentence to encourage deeper conversation
- Lower your tone when asking commitment questions (shows empathy, concern)
- Match their speaking speed and energy level

**The "Negative Question Flip" (Jeremy Miner):**
- Instead of: "Would you be open to a quick conversation?"
- Use: "Would you be completely opposed to a quick conversation?"
- The brain naturally says "no" to being opposed, so they say "no, I'm not opposed" = yes

**The "Suppose Question":**
- When you hit resistance: "Suppose we could solve [their objection]... would this be the right solution for you?"
- This isolates whether their objection is real or a smokescreen.

**Mirroring (Shelby Sapp):**
- Mirror the last 3 words of their sentence to encourage deeper conversation
- "I'm just worried about the time commitment" → "The time commitment?"
- "Yes, I don't know if I have the bandwidth" → "The bandwidth?"

| Stage | Time | Your Goal | Key Technique |
|---|---|---|---|
| Connection | 30 sec | Disarm, create curiosity | Negative question flip |
| Situation | 2-3 min | Understand current state | Probing questions |
| Problem Awareness | 2-3 min | Surface emotional pain | Provoking questions |
| Consequence | 1-2 min | Build urgency | "What happens if nothing changes?" |
| Future State | 1-2 min | Paint the dream | "In a perfect world..." |
| Transition | 30 sec | Get permission to present | "Would it make sense to show you?" |
| Presentation | 3-5 min | Show tailored solution | Address THEIR specific problems |
| Commitment | 1-2 min | Close with confidence | Grand Slam Offer structure |

### The 80/20 Rule
- **Prospect talks:** 80% of the time
- **You talk:** 20% of the time
- **If you're talking more than 20%, you're losing the sale**

### Key Tonality Tips
- Speak with periods, not commas (clear, distinct statements)
- Lower tone for commitment questions (shows empathy)
- Mirror their speed and energy
- After asking a question: SHUT UP. Silence is powerful.

### The Identity Sell
> "You seem like the kind of owner who really cares about your customers and your reputation. Most restaurant owners I talk to are just trying to get by — but you're different. You're thinking about the long game. That's exactly the kind of partner we want to work with."

## Part 14: Key Takeaways from Each Master

### Alex Hormozi's Key Lessons for BEYONDVYU
1. **The offer IS the product.** A great product with a weak offer loses to a mediocre product with a great offer.
2. **Stack value before you raise price.** Add bonuses, guarantees, and urgency before you ever discount.
3. **The guarantee is your biggest conversion lever.** "If you don't see results, it's free" is more powerful than any feature.
4. **Price is a signal of value.** If your price makes you comfortable, it's too low.
5. **Compete on value, not price.** If they're comparing you to competitors, your offer isn't differentiated enough.

### Jeremy Miner's Key Lessons for BEYONDVYU
1. **Questions > statements.** Every time you want to make a statement, turn it into a question.
2. **80/20 rule.** If you're talking more than 20%, you're losing the sale.
3. **Silence is powerful.** After asking a question, shut up. Let them fill the space.
4. **Listen to what they MEAN, not what they SAY.** Tonality reveals more than words.
5. **Never disagree, always explore.** "I understand. Tell me more about that."
6. **The Suppose Question** isolates real objections from smokescreens.

### Shelby Sapp's Key Lessons for BEYONDVYU
1. **The 3-bucket question system** — Probing, Provoking, Future State.
2. **Sell the gap, not the product.** You're selling the bridge between where they are and where they want to be.
3. **The Identity Sell** — Frame them with positive attributes they want to live up to.
4. **Speak with periods, not commas** — clear, distinct statements with downward inflection.
5. **Mirror the last 3 words** to encourage deeper conversation.
6. **Rejection is feedback, not failure.** Every "no" teaches you something.
