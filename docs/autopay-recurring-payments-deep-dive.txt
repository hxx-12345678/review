1. RBI E-MANDATE FRAMEWORK 2026
================================================================================

On April 21, 2026, RBI consolidated ALL recurring payment circulars (2019-2024)
into a single "Digital Payments — E-mandate Framework, 2026" governing cards,
UPI, and PPIs.

KEY RULES:

  1. MANDATE REGISTRATION
     - One-time process with AFA (OTP for cards, UPI PIN for UPI)
     - Mandate must specify validity period
     - Customer can modify/cancel anytime with AFA

  2. TRANSACTION LIMITS (AFA-FREE)
     - Up to ₹15,000: NO additional authentication needed
     - Up to ₹1,00,000: For insurance premiums, mutual fund SIPs,
       credit card bill payments only
     - Above these: Per-transaction AFA required

  3. PRE-DEBIT NOTIFICATION (CRITICAL CHANGE IN 2026)
     - MUST be sent 24 hours BEFORE EVERY recurring debit
     - Applies to ALL amounts (not just above ₹15K as before)
     - Must include: merchant name, amount, date/time, mandate ref,
       cancellation option
     - Customer can one-tap opt-out of any transaction

  4. POST-DEBIT NOTIFICATION
     - Mandatory after each debit
     - Must include grievance redressal contact

  5. CARD REISSUANCE
     - Existing e-mandates map to reissued cards automatically
     - No need for customers to re-register on card replacement

  6. NO CHARGES TO CUSTOMER
     - Merchants CANNOT levy charges for e-mandate facility

IMPACT ON SUBSCRIPTION BUSINESSES:
  - Universal pre-debit alert means EVERY renewal is a potential churn moment
  - Need to build value-touch communication 48-72 hours BEFORE the
    RBI-mandated alert
  - ₹15K ceiling means sub-₹1000 SaaS is in the optimal frictionless zone
  - Card reissuance continuity is a net positive

2. THE THREE RECURRING RAILS
================================================================================

2.1 UPI AUTOPAY
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  WHAT IT IS:
    Recurring e-mandate system enabling automatic debits directly from bank
    accounts via UPI apps. Bypasses card networks entirely.

  HOW IT WORKS:
    1. Customer selects UPI Autopay at checkout
    2. Enters UPI VPA and approves mandate with UPI PIN
    3. Mandate registered instantly with NPCI
    4. On billing date, Razorpay/Cashfree/PhonePe sends debit request
    5. Funds debited directly from bank account
    6. Pre-debit notification sent 24hr before (by bank/gateway)

  LIMITS:
    Standard:        ₹15,000 per debit (frictionless)
    Insurance/SIPs:  ₹1,00,000 per debit
    Education/Healthcare: Up to ₹5,00,000 (verified merchants)

  SETUP TIME:       Instant (seconds)

  SUCCESS RATE:     ~93% (Razorpay) to ~96% (PhonePe)

  COST:
    MDR:            0% (NPCI mandate — zero interchange)
    Platform Fee:   Varies by gateway (Razorpay 2%, Cashfree 1.75%,
                    Zoho 0.5%, VyaparGateway ₹0)
    Mandate Fee:    Usually bundled (Zoho charges ₹7.50 setup + ₹5 execute)

  PROS:
    + Instant mandate creation (highest conversion)
    + Zero MDR (cheapest rail)
    + Highest success rate for sub-₹15K
    + Mobile-first (great for Indian SMBs)
    + 99-year max tenure
    + No card expiry issues
    + PDN handled by gateway/bank

  CONS:
    - Domestic only (no cross-border)
    - ₹15K limit (₹1L for eligible categories)
    - Requires UPI app (not all customers use UPI)
    - 24hr PDN means customer can cancel each cycle

  BEST FOR: Sub-₹1000 SaaS subscriptions, OTT, utilities, SMB plans

2.2 CARD eMANDATES (TOKENIZED RECURRING)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  WHAT IT IS:
    Standing Instructions on credit/debit cards for recurring payments.
    Card details are tokenized (replaced with network token) per RBI mandate.

  HOW IT WORKS:
    1. Customer enters card details + consents to tokenization
    2. First transaction: OTP/3DS authentication (this creates mandate)
    3. Card is tokenized (Razorpay TokenHQ / Cashfree Token Vault)
    4. Token stored against customer profile
    5. On billing date, token + cryptogram sent to card network
    6. Funds debited from card
    7. PDN sent 24hr before each debit

  LIMITS:
    Without AFA:    ₹15,000 per debit
    With AFA:       Card network-defined (typically no cap above ₹15K)
    Max tenure:     Till card expiry (auto-maps to reissued card per 2026 rules)

  SETUP TIME:      Instant (with OTP)

  SUCCESS RATE:    ~85-90% (lower than UPI due to card expiry, bank declines)

  COST:
    MDR:            1.5-2.2% (credit) + 0.4-0.9% (debit)
    Platform Fee:   On top of MDR (Razorpay 2%, etc.)
    Tokenization:   Usually included

  PROS:
    + No practical upper limit (can charge any amount with AFA)
    + Works internationally (cross-border SaaS)
    + Customers with premium cards get rewards
    + Better for enterprise/B2B customers
    + Card reissuance continuity (2026 rules)

  CONS:
    - Higher MDR than UPI (1.5-2.2% vs 0%)
    - Card expiry causes failures (~15% of recurring card txns fail)
    - Per-transaction AFA needed above ₹15K
    - Lower success rate than UPI Autopay
    - More complex tokenization compliance
    - Chargeback risk higher than bank debit

  BEST FOR: Premium plans >₹15K, international subscriptions,
            enterprise B2B, customers without UPI

  RBI CARD RECURRING RULES (2026):
    - ₹5,000 threshold REMOVED (was there 2021-2022)
    - Now ₹15,000 uniform across cards, UPI, PPIs
    - Card tokenization mandatory (no storing raw card numbers)
    - Pre-debit notification mandatory 24hr before
    - Auto-mapping to reissued cards (new in 2026)

2.3 eNACH (BANK ACCOUNT MANDATES)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  WHAT IT IS:
    Electronic National Automated Clearing House — bank-to-bank recurring
    debit mandate. Customer authorizes merchant to debit their bank account.

  HOW IT WORKS:
    1. Merchant creates mandate request
    2. Customer authorizes via netbanking, debit card, or Aadhaar OTP
    3. Bank verifies and activates mandate (24-48 hours)
    4. On each billing date, debit request sent through NPCI's NACH system
    5. Funds debited from customer's bank account

  LIMITS:
    Standard:       Up to ₹1,00,00,000 (₹1 crore) per debit
    Setup time:     1-5 working days

  SUCCESS RATE:    ~88-95% (varies by bank)

  COST:
    Setup:          Usually ₹2-5 per transaction (passed through by gateway)
    Platform Fee:   Varies

  PROS:
    + Highest limit (₹1Cr) — suitable for EMIs, insurance, B2B
    + High success rate once mandate active
    + No card expiry issues
    + Works for all bank account holders

  CONS:
    - 1-5 day activation (not instant — high dropoff during setup)
    - Higher setup friction (netbanking login required)
    - Domestic only
    - Not suitable for sub-₹1000 txns (setup cost doesn't amortize)
    - 30-year max tenure

  BEST FOR: High-value collections >₹15K, EMIs, insurance premiums,
            B2B retainers, loan repayments

3. RAIL-BY-RAIL COMPARISON
================================================================================

  Parameter              | UPI Autopay    | Card eMandate  | eNACH
  -----------------------|----------------|----------------|----------------
  Setup Time             | Instant        | Instant        | 1-5 days
  Setup Friction         | Very Low       | Low            | Medium
  Max Limit (no AFA)     | ₹15,000        | ₹15,000        | ₹1,00,000
  Max Limit (with AFA)   | ₹1,00,000*     | No practical   | ₹1,00,00,000
  Per-Txn Cost (ex-GST)  | 0-2%           | 2-4%           | 1-2% + ₹2-5
  Success Rate           | 93-96%         | 85-90%         | 88-95%
  Card Expiry Issues     | None           | Yes (auto-map) | None
  Cross-Border           | No             | Yes            | No
  Customer Reach (India) | 500M+ UPI users| ~50M card users| All bank accs
  Max Mandate Tenure     | 99 years       | Card expiry    | 30 years
  PDN Required           | Yes (24hr)     | Yes (24hr)     | Yes (24hr)
  Best For               | Sub-₹15K txns  | Premium/Global | High-value/EMI

  *₹1L for insurance/SIPs/credit cards. Education/healthcare up to ₹5L.

6. RECOMMENDED STRATEGY FOR REVIEWOS
================================================================================

  PHASE 1 — MVP / EARLY STAGE (NOW)
  ==================================

    PRIMARY RECURRING RAIL: UPI Autopay via Razorpay

    WHY UPI AUTOPAY:
      - Sub-₹1000 txns are well within ₹15K frictionless limit
      - 0% MDR (only Razorpay's 2% platform fee = ₹23.60 on ₹1000)
      - Instant mandate creation (no dropoff during signup)
      - 93%+ success rate (best in class)
      - No card expiry issues
      - RBI 2026 compliant (PDN handled by Razorpay)

    CARD FALLBACK:
      - Razorpay Card eMandates for customers who prefer cards
      - TokenHQ handles all RBI tokenization compliance
      - Card change handled via hosted page (zero dev work)
      - Effective cost: 2% platform fee + card MDR (1.5-2.2%)
        = ~3.5-4.2% on card recurring

    IMPLEMENTATION:
      - Razorpay Subscriptions API already available
      - Create plan → Create subscription → Auto-charge
      - Webhooks: subscription.activated, subscription.charge.succeeded,
        subscription.charge.failed, subscription.halted
      - Retry: Built-in auto-retry, email to customer with card change link
      - PDN: Handled automatically by Razorpay

  PHASE 2 — GROWTH (₹3-5L/mo GMV)
  =================================

    ADD: VyaparGateway for UPI-only routing

    UPI TRAFFIC: VyaparGateway (0% platform fee, ₹300/mo flat)
    CARD TRAFFIC: Razorpay (2% platform fee + MDR)
    eNACH: Razorpay (for any future high-value plans)

    SAVINGS: ~₹8K-12K/mo on UPI fees

  PHASE 3 — SCALE (₹10L+/mo GMV)
  ================================

    NEGOTIATE: Razorpay custom pricing (1.5-1.75%)
    ADD: Stripe for international card recurring
    ALL THREE RAILS ACTIVE:
      - UPI Autopay (Razorpay or VyaparGateway)
      - Card eMandates (Razorpay + Stripe)
      - eNACH (Razorpay, for enterprise plans)
